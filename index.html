HBPB1-007

First-in-Class Topical Therapeutic Candidate for Geographic Atrophy

A 32-amino-acid TIR-derived peptide that blocks TLR signalosome assembly, dampening the broader inflammatory cascade that drives GA — delivered as a non-invasive eye drop.

Science

Mechanism of Action

HBPB1-007 intercepts TIR-TIR contacts required to nucleate the TLR signalosome, shutting down NF-κB / MAPK activation and the broader inflammatory cascade it drives.

  • Disrupts MAL–MyD88 TIR assembly. Prevents myddosome nucleation at TLR adapters.
  • Suppresses NF-κB and MAPK phosphorylation. ERK, JNK, p38 signaling attenuated in macrophages and RPE-relevant models.
  • Dampens pro-inflammatory cytokines. IL-6, IL-8, and CCL2 are reduced; downstream effects extend to the complement axis as one of several affected pathways.
Platform: Discovered via T-Surf — a massively parallel phage-display screen of TIR-domain surface peptides (Lim et al., Adv Sci 2025).
Key Data

Preclinical Evidence

Three pillars of evidence supporting HBPB1-007 as a topical GA candidate: retinal efficacy, posterior ocular delivery, and ocular safety.

1

In vivo Efficacy

NaIO₃ mouse model of RPE degeneration

  • ONL thickness significantly rescued at 20 µM TDIP5
  • Retinal IBA1⁺ microglia significantly reduced vs. control
  • Structural + immunological rescue — parallel readouts, not a single surrogate
2

Posterior Ocular Delivery

Topical instillation reaches the posterior segment

  • Retina / choroid exposure achieved after eye-drop dosing
  • Supports non-invasive alternative to intravitreal injection
  • Bridges a critical gap for GA patient compliance
3

Ocular Safety

NZW rabbit 4-week topical study (preliminary)

  • IOP within normal range (10–21 mmHg) at all time points
  • No adverse findings up to 0.5% solution (300 µL/head/day)
  • No treatment-related ocular findings observed
